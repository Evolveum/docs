This Acceptable Use Policy ("*Policy*") applies to all services hosted or operated by Evolveum s.r.o. ("*Evolveum*", "*we*", "*us*" or "*our*") through which users or third parties may submit, upload, post, or otherwise contribute content.

This Policy forms part of Evolveum's link:https://evolveum.com/terms-of-use/[Terms of Use].

== Scope

This Policy applies to:

* *Evolveum hosted services* – including content uploaded by users to Evolveum-operated environments, such as configuration files, connector definitions, role schemas, workflow definitions, and scripts (collectively "*Uploaded Configurations*"), as well as integrations and connectors published to the Integration Catalog.

* *Community Platforms* – including Evolveum's public mailing lists and discussion forums at lists.evolveum.com and any publicly accessible community discussion platform operated by Evolveum.

By contributing content to any Evolveum-hosted service, the user confirms that they have read and agree to this Policy.

== General rules – all services

These rules apply to all services hosted by Evolveum and to everyone who uses them:

* *Lawful use only.* Use the service solely for purposes permitted under applicable law, including Regulation (EU) 2022/2065 (DSA) and Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR).
* *No illegal or infringing content.* Do not upload, post, or share content that is unlawful or that infringes the intellectual property or other rights of any third party.
* *Respectful conduct.* Do not harass, threaten, defame, or discriminate against any person or group.
* *Accurate identity.* Do not impersonate any person or organisation, and keep your contact information current and accurate.
* *No interference with security.* Do not probe, exploit, or attempt to undermine the security or integrity of Evolveum's products, platforms, or access controls.

== Community platforms – mailing lists & discussion forums

This section applies to content posted to Evolveum's public mailing lists and discussion forums at lists.evolveum.com and any other publicly accessible Evolveum community platform.

=== Permitted use

Users may post:

* Technical questions, answers, bug reports, and feature discussions related to midPoint.
* Announcements relevant to the midPoint open-source community, including releases, events, and community initiatives.
* Professional discussion of identity governance, IAM standards, and related technology topics.
* Links to publicly available resources relevant to the midPoint community, provided the linked content does not itself violate this Policy.

=== Prohibited content

* *Illegal content.* Content that is unlawful under EU law or the law of any EU Member State, including content that infringes intellectual property rights, constitutes unlawful hate speech, or violates applicable data protection law.
* *Spam.* Unsolicited advertising, promotional material, or repetitive off-topic posting.
* *Malicious content.* Links to malware, phishing pages, or exploit code, or content designed to harm users or systems.
* *Sensitive information.* Personal data, credentials, API keys, passwords, or confidential information of any person or organisation, posted without the consent of the data subject or a valid legal basis under GDPR.
* *Harassment and abuse.* Personal attacks, threats, or content targeting individuals or groups based on any protected characteristic.
* *Commercial solicitation.* Advertising of products or services unrelated to the midPoint ecosystem without prior written consent from Evolveum.

[NOTE]
====
Evolveum does not pre-screen or moderate content posted to community platforms. We are not liable for user-generated content; however, upon becoming aware of illegal content, we will act expeditiously to remove or disable access to it in accordance with Article 6 of Regulation (EU) 2022/2065 (DSA).
====

== Uploaded configurations – the integration catalog

This section applies to all configuration files, connector definitions, role schemas, workflow definitions, synchronisation policies, scripts, and any other content uploaded by users to the midPoint hosted platform for publication to the Integration Catalog.

*The Integration Catalog* is a shared library enabling users to discover, reuse, and contribute community-validated midPoint connectors, accessible to all users of the platform.

Users may only upload configurations designed for legitimate identity governance and access management purposes, compatible with midPoint's published configuration schema, and in compliance with applicable law, including GDPR and applicable national legislation.

Evolveum reserves the right, at its sole discretion, to decline publication of any uploaded configuration following review, including where the configuration is duplicative of existing catalog content, does not meet quality or compatibility standards, or for any other reason Evolveum deems appropriate.

===  Prohibited configurations

==== Unauthorised access and data exfiltration

* Configurations designed to access, extract, or exfiltrate data from systems, directories, or databases without the authorisation of the
system owner.
* Connector configurations targeting third-party systems without a lawful basis for access – for example, LDAP or Active Directory connectors
pointed at directories the user does not own or control.
* Configurations that automate bulk harvesting of personal data beyond the systems the user is authorised to manage, or without a valid legal
basis under GDPR.

==== Malicious or harmful configurations

* Configurations embedding malicious scripts, shell commands, or code intended to damage systems, disrupt services, or cause harm to Evolveum services or third parties.
* Connector definitions that exploit known vulnerabilities in target systems.
* Configurations designed to disable or bypass security controls, access management policies, or audit logging on any system.

==== Illegal configurations

* Configurations that infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party, including but not limited to copyright, trade secrets, or
proprietary connector code. By uploading or publishing a configuration, the user warrants that they are the author or otherwise hold all rights
necessary to upload and, where applicable, publish it.
* Configurations that facilitate or enable any activity that is illegal under EU law or national law, including but not limited to: unauthorised
processing of special categories of personal data (Article 9 GDPR), bulk harvesting of personal data without a valid legal basis, unlawful surveillance, or identity fraud.
* Configurations that, directly or indirectly, contribute to any criminal offence, including offences involving threats to the life or safety of
any person.

==== Abuse of service controls

* Configurations that attempt to bypass the resource limits, access controls, or isolation boundaries of the hosted midPoint service.
* Configurations intentionally designed to generate excessive traffic or otherwise degrade service availability or performance for other users.

== Reporting illegal or policy-violating content

Any user or third party who believes that content hosted on any Evolveum service – whether a mailing list post, a discussion forum message, or an uploaded configuration – violates this Policy or constitutes illegal content under EU or Slovak law may submit a notice to us by electronic means:

* Email: dsa@evolveum.com (Slovak and English accepted)

The notice should include:

* (a) Identification of the specific content – for example, the URL, message ID, list name and date, or configuration file name within the service.
* (b) A clear description of why the content is considered illegal or in violation of this Policy.
* (c) The reporter’s name and contact email address (if it differs from the sender’s address).
* (d) A statement that the reporter believes in good faith that the information provided is accurate and complete.

We confirm receipt of the notice without undue delay and we inform the reporter of our decision regarding the reported content, along with information on possibilities for redress.

This reporting mechanism fulfils our obligation under Article 16 of Regulation (EU) 2022/2065 (Digital Services Act) to provide an accessible notice and action mechanism for hosted content. All notices are processed promptly, diligently, non-arbitrarily, and objectively.

== Enforcement

If we determine that user content violates this Policy or applicable law, we may – depending on the nature and severity of the violation – take one or more of the following measures:

* Remove or disable access to the specific content (mailing list post, discussion message, or uploaded configuration).
* Suspend or restrict the user's access to the affected service or upload functionality.
* Terminate the user's account or service agreement in whole or in part.

We notify affected users of any restriction without undue delay. The notification includes a clear and specific statement of the reasons for our decision – the factual circumstances on which it is based, the Policy provision or legal provision relied upon, whether automated means were used in reaching the decision, and the options available for redress.

Where we obtain credible information giving rise to a suspicion of a criminal offence involving a threat to the life or safety of any person, we notify the competent Slovak law enforcement authorities without undue delay.
